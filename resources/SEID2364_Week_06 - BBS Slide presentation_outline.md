## 1. Opening Frame and Context

- Title and framing:
  - Describing combinative reality as a tool for ethical analysis.
  - Instructor identity and institutional context.

  - Historical resistance to tools does not necessarily imply elimination of work; often transformation.

## 2. Combinative Reality Problem Statement

- People inhabit and act across multiple spaces at once.
- Realities are increasingly blended across physical and virtual contexts.
- Existing models are presented as insufficient to describe this complexity.
- Need for a framework that can differentiate and relate sub-realities.

## 5. Case Study Block: Music, Mediation, and Resistance

### 5.1 Music as a technology-mediated domain
- Instruments as historically advanced technologies of their time.
- Recording and transmission technologies disconnect music from fixed time/place:
  - Edison phonograph
  - Marconi radio

### 5.2 Claimed systemic effects of music technology
- Unlimited listening and repetition
- Decoupling musician presence from monetization
- Cost shifts and audience expansion
- New labor roles and new art forms
- New possibilities for analysis and production

### 5.3 Labor and institutional response
- Musicians' union history and strike actions across media transitions:
  - Phonograph
  - Talkies
  - Radio
  - Recording bans
  - Television studio orchestra shifts
  - Synthesizer limitations
  - Canned music disputes
  - Virtual orchestra labor disputes

### 5.4 Rhetorical and cultural resistance
- Quotes expressing concern over dehumanization and cultural decline.
- Argument pattern: technological mediation framed as civilizational threat.

### 5.5 Virtual orchestra trajectory
- First virtual orchestra references and production history.
- Hybrid orchestral implementations in theatre/performance.
- Practical framing of controversy:
  - Problem was not poor quality; concern was that mediated output could sound "too good."

### 5.6 Blended-space interpretation of orchestra
- Comparative strengths:
  - Human: nuance, interaction, uniqueness, passion
  - Virtual: consistency, scale, integration, efficiency, intonation
- Hybrid model emphasized as blended-space system:
  - Flexible instrumentation
  - Expanded repertoire
  - Adaptability
  - Integration with theatrical systems
  - Smaller footprint and economic advantages

## 15. Throughline Summary (Text-Only)

The deck's integrated argument can be summarized as:

1. Technological disruption repeatedly reshapes human systems.
2. Current AI-era conditions are increasingly combinative across physical/virtual domains.
3. Existing mixed-reality labels are inadequate for ethical/structural analysis.
4. BBS is proposed as a symmetric, relational, communication-centered framework.
5. Mediation pathways (SVD) provide the methodological core for describing system dynamics.
6. This descriptive method is necessary before robust ethical evaluation in complex human-AI-institutional systems.
