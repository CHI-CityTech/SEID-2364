Identify where your current model fails.

1. Where does your current description become unclear?

2. Which of these is the main problem? (choose one or more)

- unclear boundaries of the system
- no stable definition of source/vector/destination
- no distinction between physical and virtual agency
- information-access asymmetry (critical information visible to some actors but not others)
- hidden transformations in the pathway
- unclear transitions between physical and virtual space
- authority/responsibility diffusion

3. Write one sentence completing this prompt:

## Part 4 — Post-Lecture Pathway Reconstruction (25–30 min)

Now rebuild your system description using BBS structure.

### A. Pathway Inventory

List at least three pathways:

- Data pathway
- Decision pathway
- Accountability pathway

For each pathway, provide:

- Source
- Vector
- Destination
- What is transmitted
- Where physical agency is exercised
- Where virtual agency is exercised
- Who can access the information at each step
- What governs access (role, policy, platform permissions, legal/contractual constraint)

### B. Physical/Virtual Travel Trace

For each pathway, identify:

- physical nodes (people, places, institutions, hardware)
- virtual nodes (platforms, models, APIs, databases, interfaces)
- transitions between physical and virtual nodes

### C. Transformation and Friction

For each pathway, identify one item for each:

- transformation point
- access-control point (where visibility or permission changes)
- opacity point
- authority/responsibility divergence
- distance increase
- missing or weak feedback loop

## Part 5 — Quick Schematic (10–15 min)

Draw one pathway map by hand or digitally.

Required elements:

- at least 5 nodes
- arrows with direction
- at least 1 physical-to-virtual transition
- labels for physical agency and virtual agency
- one marked friction point

Attach this to your notes or embed it later in your Assignment 6 report.
