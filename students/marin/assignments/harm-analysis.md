# SEID 2364 – Assignment 3: Harm Framework Analysis

## Purpose

In previous sessions we examined two foundational ideas:

- **Ethics** – how actions should be evaluated
- **Intelligence** – how systems make decisions and act

If a system is capable of making decisions, it can produce actions. Actions produce consequences, and those consequences may involve harm or benefit.

Before ethical frameworks can be applied, we must first identify and analyze the harms that may arise from actions within a system.

This assignment introduces the analytical problem of identifying and classifying harm.

A list of potential types of harm can be found in the GitHub repository here: [Comprehensive Types of Harm](https://github.com/CHI-CityTech/SEID-2364/blob/main/resources/03-SEID2364_Comprehensive_Types_of_Harm.md)

## Part 2 – Core Readings

Consult the following readings in the Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy (SEP). You are not required to read the entire article. Focus on the sections indicated.

### Reading 1 – The Harm Principle

**Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy** – [The Harm Principle](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/harm-principle/)

**Read:**
- Introduction
- Section 1 (Mill's formulation of the harm principle)

This reading introduces the idea that many ethical systems focus on preventing harm to others.

### Reading 2 – Moral Responsibility

**Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy** – [Moral Responsibility](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/moral-responsibility/)

**Read:**
- Introduction
- Section 1 (basic concept of responsibility)

This reading explores why responsibility matters when actions affect other people.

### Reading 3 – Ethical Frameworks

**Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy** – [Normative Ethics](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/ethics-normative/)

**Read:**
- Introduction

This article introduces the idea that societies develop ethical frameworks (such as consequentialism, deontology, and virtue ethics) in order to evaluate actions and their consequences.
