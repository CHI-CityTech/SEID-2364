1.	When no option avoids harm, choosing the right action means minimizing overall harm and choosing the most ethically justifiable outcome, while considering fairness, intentions, and consequences.

2.	Example:
A doctor has one dose of a life-saving drug but two patients need it.
Person A thinks giving it to the younger patient is right because they have more years to live  minimizes long-term harm. Person B thinks giving it to the sicker patient is right because they are in immediate danger reduces urgent harm.

3.  People can decide between competing harms in different ways:
Minimize total harm, Prioritize urgency, Fairness/equality, Rights and rules, Long-term impact
