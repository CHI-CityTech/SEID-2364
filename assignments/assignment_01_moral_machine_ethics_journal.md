## Assignment Purpose

This first assignment introduces ethical analysis through a large-scale public dataset of moral judgments. You will examine response patterns in the Moral Machine dataset and use those findings to sharpen your own ethical framing.

This assignment has two connected parts:

1. **Dataset analysis:** Evaluate how to use large-group moral response data to discuss ethics.
2. **Journal entry 1:** Record your exact first prompts from class and refine your ethical framing questions.

---

## Learning Goals

By completing this assignment, you will be able to:

- Interpret aggregate ethical response data from many participants.
- Distinguish descriptive patterns (what people chose) from normative claims (what is right).
- Identify limits and risks in using crowd moral data for ethical reasoning.
- Begin an explicit personal ethics journal that documents your starting assumptions.

## Part A — Moral Machine Dataset Analysis

Prepare a short analysis (900–1200 words or equivalent structured report) addressing the following:

1. **What the dataset represents**
   - What kind of ethical responses are recorded?
   - Who appears to be represented, and who may be underrepresented?

2. **Pattern identification**
   - Identify at least 2–3 notable response patterns from the data or provided results.
   - Explain what each pattern suggests about group-level ethical preference.

3. **Interpretive caution**
   - What can this dataset support as evidence?
   - What can it not support?
   - Where might aggregation hide conflict, bias, or cultural differences?

4. **Ethics of using the dataset**
   - Should crowd response data influence policy or system behavior?
   - Under what constraints or safeguards?
