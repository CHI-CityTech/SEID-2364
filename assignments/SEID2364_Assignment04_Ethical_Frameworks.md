## Purpose

In previous work, you identified and analyzed harm within systems.

However, identifying harm is not sufficient.

In real-world situations, different individuals and groups often disagree about:
- what counts as harm  
- which harms matter most  
- how competing harms should be resolved  

These disagreements arise because decisions are structured through different ethical frameworks.

This assignment shifts the focus:

> Not just *what is harm*, but *how do we decide what to do when harms conflict?*

You will investigate ethical frameworks, apply them to a system, and critically evaluate whether they are sufficient for analyzing harm in complex, multi-factor environments.

### 3B – Framework Evaluation (Critical Analysis)

Evaluate this framework as a system for analyzing harm.

Rather than answering yes/no questions, you must analyze how the framework performs across the following dimensions:

- How clearly does the framework define what counts as harm?  
- How does it structure decision-making when multiple actions are possible?  
- How does it handle situations involving multiple stakeholders with conflicting interests?  
- How does it compare or prioritize different types of harm (e.g., economic vs. social vs. psychological)?  
- To what extent can it scale from individual decisions to large systems (e.g., platforms, AI systems)?  
- What kinds of harm does the framework tend to ignore, minimize, or exclude?  

You must include at least one concrete scenario where the framework produces a questionable, incomplete, or ambiguous result.
