## Framing

In Week 11, you encountered the COVID-19 misinformation moderation case through the class provocations. You now have the expanded case study, which gives a fuller account of the platforms, public-health institutions, technical systems, users, and contexts involved.

Use that shared case to evaluate one misinformation intervention strategy through two professional codes of conduct:

- the ACM Code of Ethics and Professional Conduct;
- the IEEE Code of Ethics.

Choose **one intervention strategy** from the expanded case study and evaluate how ACM and IEEE would apply their professional principles to the people designing, implementing, maintaining, or evaluating that strategy.

Your task is not to decide whether COVID-19 misinformation moderation was simply right or wrong. Your task is to compare how two professional ethical frameworks apply their principles to the same intervention strategy.

The central question is:

> How do ACM and IEEE define responsible professional conduct differently when applied to the same misinformation intervention strategy?

This assignment focuses on professional ethical responsibility. Keep your attention on how professional codes guide ethical conduct.

## Required Case Study

Use the expanded case study:

**[COVID-19 Misinformation Moderation: A Comparative Case Study in Platform Governance](../case-studies/case_study-covid_misinformation.md)**

The case describes how social media platforms, public-health institutions, automated moderation tools, human reviewers, fact-checkers, users, and governments interacted during the COVID-19 pandemic. It includes tensions around misinformation, public health, scientific uncertainty, platform moderation, visibility, free expression, institutional trust, and accountability.

The case also describes different intervention strategies and different governance contexts. You should briefly identify the governance context relevant to your chosen intervention strategy, but you are not being asked to compare governments in this assignment. Governmental authority and regulatory governance will be taken up later. Here, the expanded case study provides the shared factual and ethical context for comparing ACM and IEEE as professional ethical frameworks.

Use this case study as your primary case material. Do not conduct major additional research into COVID-19 policy.
